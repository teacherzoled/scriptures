Welcome! What's in a Name?

Hello, everyone! Did you know that just like you have a special name, God has many names too? These names aren't just labels; they are like secret keys that unlock who He is and how much He loves us!

Think about your own name. Maybe it means "strong," "brave," or "gift from God." Our names tell a story about us. In the same way, God's names tell us His amazing story.

Let's Learn God's Special Names

Let's explore some of God's names. As we learn each one, think about how you can call on Him using that name in your life here in beautiful Belize!

Elohim

The Strong Creator God

This is the first name for God we see in the Bible! It means He is the all-powerful God who created everything‚Äîfrom the huge barrier reef to the tiny hummingbird. When you see a beautiful sunset over the sea, you're seeing the work of Elohim.

"In the beginning, God (Elohim) created the heavens and the earth." (Genesis 1:1)

üôè Prayer Starter: "Thank You, Elohim, for creating me and our beautiful country of Belize."

Abba

Father

This is a very special and personal name for God. "Abba" is like calling Him "Daddy." It shows how close and loving He is. Just like the father in the story of the lost son, God is always waiting with open arms to welcome us home. He loves you unconditionally!

"But while he was still a long way off, his father saw him and was filled with compassion for him; he ran to his son, threw his arms around him and kissed him." (Luke 15:20)

üôè Prayer Starter: "Thank You, Abba, for being my Father who loves me no matter what."

Jehovah Jireh

The Lord Will Provide

Are you ever worried about a test at school or if your family will have enough? Jehovah Jireh means God sees what we need and provides for us. He is our provider, just like a loving parent who makes sure we have food, like rice and beans, and a safe home.

"Abraham looked up and there in a thicket he saw a ram caught by its horns. He went over and took the ram and sacrificed it as a burnt offering instead of his son. So Abraham called that place The Lord Will Provide (Jehovah Jireh)." (Genesis 22:13-14)

üôè Prayer Starter: "Jehovah Jireh, please provide for my family and help me not to worry."

Yahweh Roi

The Lord is My Shepherd

üêë A shepherd takes care of his sheep, leading them to good food and protecting them from danger. When you feel lost, scared, or alone, remember that Yahweh Roi is your Shepherd. He guides you and protects you, whether you're at home, school, or playing with friends.

"The Lord (Yahweh) is my shepherd (Roi), I lack nothing. He makes me lie down in green pastures, he leads me beside quiet waters, he refreshes my soul." (Psalm 23:1-3)

üôè Prayer Starter: "Yahweh Roi, thank You for being my Shepherd and always watching over me."

Jehovah Rophe

The God Who Heals

When you get a cut, your body heals. When you are sad, a hug can help heal your heart. Jehovah Rophe is the God who heals us, both inside and out. He can mend our bodies when we are sick and comfort our hearts when we are hurting.

"There the Lord made a decree and a law for them, and there he tested them. He said, 'If you listen carefully to the Lord your God... I will not bring on you any of the diseases I brought on the Egyptians, for I am the Lord, who heals you (Jehovah Rophe).'" (Exodus 15:25-26)

üôè Prayer Starter: "Jehovah Rophe, please bring healing to me and those I know who are sick or sad."

El Roi

The God Who Sees Me

Have you ever felt like no one understands what you're going through? Maybe you were treated unfairly or felt invisible. El Roi is the name for God that reminds us He sees everything. He sees your secret tears and your hidden worries. You are never alone.

"She gave this name to the Lord who spoke to her: 'You are the God who sees me (El Roi),' for she said, 'I have now seen the One who sees me.'" (Genesis 16:13)

üôè Prayer Starter: "El Roi, thank You for seeing me when I feel lonely or sad."

Adonai

Lord, Master

Calling God "Adonai" is like saying He is the boss or the captain of our lives. It shows we respect Him and want to follow His good and perfect plans for us. When we obey our parents and teachers, we are also practicing what it means to have Adonai as our Lord.

"You are my Lord (Adonai); apart from you I have no good thing." (Psalm 16:2)

üôè Prayer Starter: "Adonai, help me to obey you and trust that you are in charge of my life."

Yahweh Tsuri

The Lord My Rock

üßó In Belize, we have strong Maya mountains and solid rocks. A rock is strong, steady, and a safe place to stand. Yahweh Tsuri means God is our Rock. He is our strength and our defense. When things feel shaky, we can depend on Him because He never changes.

"Praise be to the Lord my Rock (Yahweh Tsuri), who trains my hands for war, my fingers for battle." (Psalm 144:1)

üôè Prayer Starter: "Yahweh Tsuri, thank you for being my Rock when I feel afraid."

Yahweh Nissi

The Lord My Banner

Think of the Belizean flag flying high! A banner or flag shows who you belong to and represents victory. Yahweh Nissi means God is our banner of victory. With Him on our side, we can have victory over sin and fear. He is the one we look to and follow.

"Moses built an altar and called it The Lord is my Banner (Yahweh Nissi)." (Exodus 17:15)

üôè Prayer Starter: "Yahweh Nissi, thank you for giving me victory over my challenges."

Jehovah-M'Kaddesh

The God Who Sanctifies

To "sanctify" means to make something holy or special for God's purpose. We all make mistakes (sin), but God is so loving that He cleanses us and sets us apart to be His children. Jehovah-M'Kaddesh makes us special and helps us live in a way that pleases Him.

"Consecrate yourselves and be holy, because I am the Lord your God. Keep my decrees and follow them. I am the Lord, who makes you holy (Jehovah-M‚ÄôKaddesh)." (Leviticus 20:7-8)

üôè Prayer Starter: "Jehovah-M'Kaddesh, please make my heart clean and help me to be more like you."
